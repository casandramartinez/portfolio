Tot's Towers: A Children's Counting Toy

Skills: Product Development, Solidworks CAD, Design Iteration

Course: Graphics and Design MECE E3408

Tot's Towers is a children's toy designed to help children ages 4 and up learn how to count. Totâ€™s Towers is composed primarily of two tower beams with an accompanying ruler, along with the 9 blocks of different heights for stacking. The block representing 1 is 10 mm tall, the 2 block is 20 mm tall, the 3 block is 30 mm tall, and so forth. To add values together, children simply stack together the quantities on one tower and move the arrow mechanism to read the resulting value. To subtract, children will place the two quantities they are subtracting on the two distinct towers, placing their respective rulers at their respective heights. Using the platforms in between the two towers, children will be able to calculate the difference between the two numbers by finding the block that fits perfectly between the platforms. Simple instructions are embossed in the back of the toy for accessibility.

Tot's Towers underwent several iteration stages with feedback from target age children at each stage. Our first design consisted of one single tower, but children faced issues understanding how subtracting would work. This necessitated the inclusion of a second tower, which allowed kids to understand the concept of finding the difference between two values. Our final design is conveniently simple for children to understand and for a company to manufacture. Watch the video below for a full description of functionality, target market analysis, and safety evaluation of Tot's Towers.

This project was co-designed with one other mechanical engineering student. Design and development was split evenly between both parties.
