Thermoacoustic Cooling Device

Skills: Research and development, Experimental Setups, Manufacturing, System IntegrationS

Course: Senior Design MECE E3420

As part of Columbia's Senior Design course, a team of four other senior mechanical engineering students and I are designing a thermoacoustic experimental setup and device for use in data centers. Inspired by the significant role of air conditioning on the current climate crisis, we are designing a thermoacoustic device that takes input heat to drive a fundamental frequency through a tube, which then creates a pressure differential across a stack of low thermal conductivity embedded within the tube. This pressure differntial creates a "hot" and "cold" region, which can subsequently be harnessed into energy-efficient cooling.

This semester, I primarily researched prior experimental setups and possible optimization of the stack via either a spiral or parellel plate configurations. I fabricated an early iteration of the device's pressurized vessel for our initial experimental setup (see to right) using CNC lathes and nylon 3D printing, and I utilized thermistors to measure temperature readings throughout the vessel. For next semester, our team is looking to continue optimizing our initial design and begin testing in New York data centers. Stay tuned for more updates!
