Hi, welcome to a collection of my past and current work!

My name is Casandra, and I am a senior undergraduate at Columbia University pursuing a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science. I am passionate about the intersection of nuclear energy development and aerospace systems, each of which depends on each other for technical advancements.

During my studies, I have pursued several technical projects as an active member of the Columbia Space Initiative, working as a mechanical engineer team member in the Micro-g mission and leading the RASC-AL mission. I work as a research assistant at the Columbia Plasma Physics Laboratory, delivering mechanical solutions to challenges in the development of nuclear reactors. As a lover of rapid prototyping and novel manufacturing techniques, I work as a Superuser at Columbiaâ€™s Mechanical Engineering Lab, able to pursue my passion for making my ideas come to life.

As a first-generation Mexican-American student, I owe my pursuit of higher education to the Questbridge National College Match and the Gates Scholarship. My work is done in the spirit of bringing STEM education to historically excluded communities. My time at Columbia, aside from my technical pursuits, has been defined by my time with Casa Latina: a special-interest community for Latine students. In my free time, I enjoy reading science fiction novels, going on runs in Riverside Park, and camping out for Saturday Night Live tapings.
