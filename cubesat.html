Control Display for LIONESS CubeSat

Skills: Human-Centered Design, UI/UX, Aerospace Control Systems, Testing

Course: Aerospace Human Factors Engineering MECE E4811

Completed as part of former astronaut Professor Micheal Massimino's course, I ideated, designed, and tested a flight control display for the Columbia Space Initiative's LIONESS CubeSat. The Line Imaging Orbiter for Nanosatellite-Enabled Spectrographic Surveys (LIONESS) is a 6U CubeSat equipped with a spectrograph to provide insight into galaxy formation, which will be deployed in 2027 as part of NASA's Cubesat Launch Initiative. I researched other common CubeSat system displays, most notably the Mission Operation System Tool (MOST) developed at the Hawaii Space Flight Laboratory. From this background information, the team decided to test two primary display features: single v. double screen and command input methods. In the end, I created four displays, each of which was tested within CubeSat team members using a modified Cooper-Harper scale. The final design displays the CubeSat's mode (safe or experimental), charging status, temperature gradient, incoming spectrographic data, location, and orientation of the satellite.
