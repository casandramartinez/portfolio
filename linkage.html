About Me

Background

Hi! My name is Casandra, and I am a senior undergraduate at Columbia University pursuing a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering with a minor in Computer Science. As a first-generation Mexican-American student, I owe my pursuit of higher education to the Questbridge National College Match and the Gates Scholarship. My work is done in the spirit of bringing STEM education to historically excluded communities.

My time at Columbia, aside from my technical pursuits, has been defined by my time with Casa Latina: a special-interest community for Latine students. In my time, I have played an integral part in coordinating cultural events, fundraisers, and hosting career workshops for the Latine community in campus and across Harlem.

Experiences and Motivation

During my studies, I have pursued several aerospace projects as a member of the Columbia Space Initiative, working as a mechanical engineer team member in the Micro-g mission and leading the RASC-AL (Revolutionary Aerospace System Concepts - Academic Linkage) mission. This year’s RASC-AL project has involved dynamic energy systems and lunar rover prospecting, and we have collaborated with the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST) to create a lunar architecture for mass transportation of lunar volatiles.

As a lover of rapid prototyping and novel manufacturing techniques, I work as a Superuser at Columbia’s Mechanical Engineering Lab, able to pursue my passion for making my ideas come to life. In my free time, I like to read science fiction novels, go on runs in Riverside Park, and camp out for Saturday Night Live tapings. Welcome to a collection of some of my past and current work.
