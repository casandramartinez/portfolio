Autonomous Astronaut Rescue Boat

Skills: Engineering design, iteration, system integration

My Role: Mechanical Engineering Team Member

My sophomore year, I worked with the Columbia Space Initiative's Micro-g team to conceptualize, design, and fabricate an autonomous rescue boat for astronauts. In the event of a contingency landing, our design allowed responding recovery forces to access required on-scene capsule hazard information prior to committing personnel into a hazard zone. Our design uses a dual-loop Adcock antenna array to locate a swept-tone emergency signal and subsequently deliver emergency supplies to stranded astronauts.

My effors were primarily focused on the boat's multihull design, which ensured the vehicle's stability in unpredictable ocean waves. The primary boat structure was designed on Solidworks and an early prototype was 3D printed using PLA and SLS. I also directed the boat's propulsion design, which utilized two reversible motors on the rear of the boat, allowing for full 2D steering capability.

When the challenge concluded, our team looked for different ways to apply our design and ultimately turned to environmental applications. The team worked closely with Columbia's Lamont Doherty Earth Observatory to adapt our design to detect algae levels in the nearby Morningside Park's articifial pond. I primarily worked on the design of the boat's sampling mechanism, using a syringe-like attachment to filter large debris from samples. The device is currently being tested in Morningside Park.
